Everything You Need to Know

Find answers to common questions about Schengen visa applications.

How long does a Schengen visa take to process?

Standard processing takes 15-21 business days from the date of your embassy appointment. However, processing times vary by country — some consulates may process faster (10-15 days) while others may take longer (up to 30 days). We recommend applying at least 3 weeks before your planned travel date to account for any delays.

Can I apply for multiple European countries with one Schengen visa?

Yes! A single Schengen visa allows you to travel across all 27 Schengen countries including France, Germany, Italy, Spain, Greece, Netherlands, and more. The visa is valid for short stays up to 90 days within a 180-day period. You can enter through any Schengen country and travel freely within the zone.

What is the validity period of a Schengen visa?

Schengen visas are typically valid for 1 to 5 years depending on your profile and the country you apply through. Multi-entry visas allow multiple trips within the validity period. The maximum stay is 90 days within any 180-day period. Some countries also offer long-term multiple-entry visas valid for up to 5 years.

Do I need travel insurance for a Schengen visa?

Yes, travel insurance is mandatory for all Schengen visa applications. You need insurance with minimum coverage of €30,000 (approximately £26,000) that covers medical emergencies, hospitalisation, and repatriation. The insurance must be valid for all Schengen countries and cover your entire stay in Europe.

Can I work in Europe with a Schengen visa?

No, a Schengen tourist visa does not allow you to work in Europe. For employment, you need a separate work permit or national visa from the specific country. If you're planning to work, study, or stay long-term in Europe, you must apply for the appropriate national visa from that country's embassy.

What happens if my Schengen visa is rejected?

If rejected, you'll receive a written reason from the embassy. Common reasons include incomplete documentation, insufficient financial proof, unclear travel purpose, or previous visa violations. You can reapply after addressing the issues. Our team helps ensure your application is complete and correctly documented to maximise approval chances.

How far in advance should I apply for a Schengen visa?

You can apply up to 6 months before your planned travel date. We recommend applying at least 3-4 weeks before your appointment date to allow for processing. For peak seasons (summer, holidays), apply even earlier as appointment slots fill up quickly. Our team helps secure your appointment within 10-15 minutes of booking!

Can I extend my Schengen visa while in Europe?

Rarely. Extensions are only possible in exceptional circumstances (force majeure, humanitarian reasons). You must apply before your visa expires through the local immigration authority in the country where you're staying. Regular tourist visas cannot be extended for leisure purposes.

What documents do I need for a Schengen visa?

Key documents include: Valid passport (6+ months), completed application form, recent photos, travel insurance (€30,000+), flight itinerary, hotel bookings, proof of finances (bank statements 3+ months), employment letter, and cover letter. Requirements vary by country.

Which Schengen country has the highest approval rate?

Approval rates vary by country and your profile. Generally, countries like Finland, Iceland, and Poland have higher approval rates. Other popular countries with good rates include Germany, France, Italy, and Spain. The key is applying to the right country based on your travel plans and documentation.

Can I book flights and hotels before getting my visa?

Yes, but book refundable options. We recommend booking flexible/refundable flights and hotels since visa outcomes aren't guaranteed. Many applicants book "reservation" style tickets that can be cancelled later. We can help arrange valid booking proofs for your application that satisfy visa requirements without financial commitment.

Do I need to attend an interview?

Usually no. Most Schengen visa applications don't require an interview. However, the embassy may call you for additional questions if needed. You'll also need to provide biometrics (fingerprints and photo) at the VFS Global centre.
